I am currently a senior at Brown University, majoring in mathematics and history. My research is in astrophysics and I am particulary interested in atmospheric dynamics on Mars. Start scrolling and learn more about me.

About Me

I am graduating from Brown University with an BA in Mathematics and in History in May of 2015. Currently, I am working with Dr. Huiqun Wang at the Harvard-Smithsonian Center for Astrophysics to study dust lifting on Mars.

Outside of acadmeics, I am actively involved in several math outreach programs. At Brown, I coordinate the Math Departmental Undergraduate Group (DUG) and the MathWiSE (Women in Science and Engineering).

Research Experience

Stanford/Lockheed Martin Solar and Astrophysics Laboratory Experience for Undergraduates (REU)

Summer 2014

Worked with Drs. Mark Cheung and Juan Martinez-Sykora to analyze our Sun's spectral data from the Interface Region Imaging Spectograph (IRIS). Studied Doppler velocities and spectral line widths for the quiet Sun in the transition region.

Harvard-Smithsonian Astrophysical Observatory Research Experience for Undergraduates (REU)

Summer 2013

Worked with Dr. Huiqun Wang to construct a database of locations of active dust lifting on Mars from existing Mars Daily Global Maps. Assessed the spatial, temporal, and size distribution of lifting areas and classified lifting events by structure.

Brown University Department of Physics

2012-2013

Studied the orientation and mobility of the 1P electron bubble in liquid helium under the guidance of Professor Humphrey Maris.

Yale University School of Engineering and Applied Science

Summer 2010

Aided Professor Daniel Rosner in completing his manuscript "Thermo-diffusion Effects in Combustion for Power Production or Chemical Synthesis."
